1. Opinion Essay (Agree / Disagree)

Question words:

Do you agree or disagree?

To what extent do you agree or disagree?

Task:
Give your opinion clearly and support it with reasons and examples.

Example:

Some people think online education is more effective than classroom teaching.
Do you agree or disagree?

Typical structure:

Introduction (paraphrase + opinion)

Body Paragraph 1 (reason)

Body Paragraph 2 (reason)

Conclusion (restate opinion)

2. Discussion Essay (Discuss Both Views)

Question words:

Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

Task:
Discuss both sides, then state your opinion.

Example:

Some people believe children should be taught at home, while others think they should attend school.
Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

Structure:

Introduction

Body 1: View A

Body 2: View B

Body 3 (or integrated): Your opinion

Conclusion

3. Advantage / Disadvantage Essay

Question words:

What are the advantages and disadvantages?

Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?

Task:
Explain benefits and drawbacks, or decide which is stronger.

Example:

Working from home has become popular.
What are the advantages and disadvantages?

Structure:

Introduction

Body 1: Advantages

Body 2: Disadvantages

Conclusion

4. Problem / Solution Essay

Question words:

What problems does this cause?

What are the possible solutions?

Task:
Explain the problem(s) and suggest solutions.

Example:

Many cities are facing serious traffic congestion.
What are the causes of this problem and what solutions can you suggest?

Structure:

Introduction

Body 1: Problems / causes

Body 2: Solutions

Conclusion

5. Double Question Essay (Two-part Question)

Question words:

Two different questions in one task

Task:
Answer both questions fully.

Example:

Many people prefer to shop online.
Why is this happening? Is this a positive or negative development?

Structure:

Introduction

Body 1: Answer Question 1

Body 2: Answer Question 2

Conclusion

6. Cause / Effect Essay (sometimes combined with solutions)

Question words:

What are the causes?

What effects does this have?

Example:

The number of people suffering from stress is increasing.
What are the causes and effects of this problem?
